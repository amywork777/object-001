## Decision: Minimal Collectible Retro Handheld

**The product:** A Game Boy Micro-sized handheld gaming device, designed entirely by AI, limited run of 10–20 numbered units. Runs open-source retro emulation firmware. Premium materials. Ships as a collector object.

## Core Design Principles

1. **Pocket-first** — fits in a jeans pocket. No compromise on this.
2. **Aluminum or premium plastic shell** — not the hollow plastic feel of cheap emulators
3. **4:3 IPS screen** (~2.8–3.0") — correct aspect ratio for retro games, no letterboxing
4. **D-pad + ABXY + shoulders + start/select** — classic layout, nothing extra
5. **USB-C charging** — modern standard only
6. **No speaker grille on front** — clean face, stereo speakers on back or bottom
7. **Numbered engraving** — each unit physically marked

## Target Specs (for manufacturing reference)

- **SoC:** Rockchip RK3566 or similar (same class as Miyoo Flip, proven)
- **RAM:** 1GB LPDDR4
- **Storage:** 16GB eMMC + microSD slot
- **Screen:** 2.8" IPS, 640×480, 60Hz
- **Battery:** 2500mAh (4–6hr gameplay)
- **OS:** Linux-based, MuOS or custom Garlic OS fork
- **Controls:** ALPS or OMRON tactile switches for buttons (premium feel)
- **Shell:** CNC aluminum top shell, polycarbonate bottom, matte finish

## What Makes It OBJECT 001

- Designed by AI — the Vizcom renders are part of the story
- Numbered limited edition (10–20 units)
- Each unit ships with a card showing its design lineage (from AI prompt → render → physical)
- The name is the brief: it's the first object this AI has made

## What It Is NOT

- Not a competitor to Steam Deck or ROG Ally
- Not another cheap Anbernic clone
- Not trying to win on specs
- Not a commercial product — this is a proof of concept and art object
